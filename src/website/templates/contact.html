Hanna Schulz

Born in Parker, Hanna's distinct artistic aesthetic was shaped by her years in Fort Colins and Australia. In her youth, Hanna was always drawn to the arts; ballet, music, dancing, woodwork, jewelry-making, and painting were some of the many talents she would acquire. After returning from Australia, where she explored new mediums of shell and sand in her paintings, Hanna moved to Denver. There she is an active member of the underground art movement, and can be found at many local art festivals.
Through skillful craft and novel approaches, Hanna shares with us her unique and expressive vision in painting. Her work often inspires one to deep thought: sometimes a detail unnoticed in a dozen viewings that changes the message of a painting, other times a work whose message leaves a contemplative thought which sits on one's mind. Her work is deeply personal; it is hard to predict how each person will be affected before they experience it.
